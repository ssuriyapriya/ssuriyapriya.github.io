Patient Length of Stay Prediction

Optimizing Resource Allocation via Machine Learning
Jun – Aug 2025 Python, SQL, XGBoost, Power BI Role: Data Science Intern

The Challenge: Unpredictability

Hospitals worldwide face significant challenges in managing bed capacity and resources due to the unpredictability of patient Length of Stay (LOS). Variations in patient volume and arrival patterns lead to overcrowding, suboptimal resource allocation, and increased healthcare costs.

Goal: "To build a machine learning model that accurately predicts LOS based on initial diagnosis, severity of illness, and treatment plans, enabling proactive resource allocation."

Data Engineering & Feature Selection

We analyzed a comprehensive dataset covering patient demographics, admission types, and clinical severity.

Multicollinearity Check (VIF)

To ensure model stability, we performed a Variance Inflation Factor (VIF) analysis to detect and remove highly correlated variables. This step was crucial for isolating the true drivers of LOS.

Selected Features

  • Clinical: Severity of Illness, Severity Scores, CCSR Diagnosis Code & Description.
  • Operational: Type of Admission (Emergency vs. Elective), Treatment Type.
  • Demographics: Patient Age, Gender.

Modeling Strategy: XGBoost

We treated this as a regression problem and evaluated multiple algorithms. Extreme Gradient Boosting (XGBoost) was selected as the optimal model due to its ability to handle non-linear relationships between diagnosis complexity and stay duration.

Performance Metrics

  • Accuracy: Achieved a prediction accuracy of 93.3%.
  • AUC Score: 0.84, indicating strong discriminatory power.
  • Hyperparameter Tuning: Optimized learning rate and tree depth to prevent overfitting on the training data.

The scatter plot (Figure 3) demonstrates a strong positive correlation between Actual and Predicted LOS, validating the model's reliability for clinical use.

Visualization & Strategic Impact

To make the model insights accessible to hospital administrators, we built an interactive Power BI Dashboard. This tool allows stakeholders to visualize bed occupancy trends and anticipate discharge bottlenecks.

Business Value

  • Resource Allocation: Hospitals can now allocate nursing staff and beds proactively based on predicted discharges.
  • Patient Categorization: Patients are categorized into "Low LOS" (likely to discharge soon) vs "High LOS" (complex care needs), enabling tailored care pathways.
  • Cost Efficiency: Reducing LOS variance directly correlates to reduced operational costs and improved patient throughput.
