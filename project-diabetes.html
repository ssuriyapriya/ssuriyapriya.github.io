Diabetes Readmission Risk Assessment

A Clinical Decision Support System (CDSS)
Spring 2025 Python, SQL, Streamlit Role: Project Lead & Data Scientist

Executive Summary & Problem Context

Hospital readmissions among diabetic patients present a persistent challenge to healthcare systems, with national rates exceeding 11.7%. These avoidable returns strain nursing capacity, incur financial penalties under Medicare's HRRP (up to 3% of reimbursements), and expose patients to hospital-acquired complications.

Currently, physicians rely on subjective judgment to assess readmission risk, leading to inconsistent discharge planning. Important indicators like HbA1c levels and recent medication changes are often underutilized in the decision-making process.

The Gap: "Identifying high-risk diabetic patients before discharge remains a major gap in healthcare delivery... existing EHR tools are general-purpose and lack diabetes-specific granularity."

IT Solution Architecture

We designed a comprehensive IT ecosystem to bridge this gap. The solution integrates a Machine Learning Microservice with a user-friendly Streamlit Interface and a robust PostgreSQL Database.

Database Schema Design

The backend relies on a relational database with four primary tables to ensure data integrity and longitudinal tracking:

  • patient_info: Stores static demographics (Age, Gender, Weight Availability).
  • visit_data: Captures dynamic utilization metrics (Inpatient visits, Lab procedures, Time in hospital).
  • predictions: Logs every model inference with a timestamp and probability score for auditability.
  • users: Manages secure authentication for clinicians.

Machine Learning Methodology

Using the "Diabetes 130-US Hospitals" dataset (1999-2008), we developed a predictive model to classify patients as "High Risk" (<30 days readmission) or "Low Risk".

Data Preprocessing & Feature Engineering

  • Exclusion Criteria: Removed records associated with patient expiration (death) to focus on preventable readmissions.
  • Imputation: Handled missing values for 'race', 'payer_code', and 'medical_specialty' using domain-aware imputation strategies.
  • Encoding: Applied One-Hot Encoding to categorical variables and mapped age intervals to numerical representatives.

Model Selection & Performance

We rigorously evaluated three algorithms: Logistic Regression, Random Forest, and Gradient Boosting.

Interface Design (Streamlit)

The frontend was built using Streamlit to ensure a lightweight, low-latency experience for busy clinicians. The design prioritizes "Actionable Intelligence" over raw data.

Key Features:

  • Real-Time Prediction: Clinicians input 12 key features (e.g., meds, lab counts) and receive an instant Readmission Probability Score.
  • Patient Lookup: Retrieval of historical patient profiles to track risk progression over multiple visits.
  • Analytics Dashboard: Aggregate-level visualizations (Histograms, Heatmaps) to help hospital administrators identify systemic trends in the patient population.

Sensitivity Analysis & Strategic Impact

To validate the model's robustness, we conducted a "What-If" sensitivity analysis. We simulated variations in hospital utilization to observe the model's response.

"Patient 1303, with the highest utilization (8 inpatient, 10 emergency visits), had a 57.49% readmission probability, crossing the high-risk threshold."

Key Findings

  • Length of Stay Impact: Sensitivity analysis indicated an increase in readmission probability of ~2.1% for every day decrease in length of stay (premature discharge risk).
  • Utilization: Prior inpatient and emergency visits were the strongest predictors of future risk.
  • Demographics: Younger age groups (20-30) showed unexpectedly higher readmission rates compared to the mean.

Implementation & Policy (PEIT Framework)

Deploying AI in healthcare requires more than just code. We developed a comprehensive PEIT (People, Environment, Information, Technology) implementation strategy.

  • People: Addressed "Alert Fatigue" by calibrating thresholds to ensure only truly high-risk patients trigger notifications.
  • Process: Defined clear SLAs for data ingestion and model inference to fit within the hospital discharge workflow.
  • Policy (HIPAA): Implemented role-based access control (RBAC) and data encryption to ensure full compliance with patient privacy regulations.
  • Technology: Proposed a containerized deployment (Docker/Kubernetes) for scalability across hospital networks.
