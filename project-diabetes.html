Diabetes Readmission Risk Assessment

Clinical Decision Support System (CDSS)
Spring 2025 Python, SQL, Streamlit Role: Project Lead

Executive Summary

[cite_start]Hospital readmissions among diabetic patients present a critical challenge, with national rates exceeding 11.7%[cite: 17]. [cite_start]These avoidable returns strain nursing capacity, incur financial penalties under Medicare's HRRP (up to 3% of reimbursements), and expose patients to hospital-acquired complications[cite: 45].

The Problem: Currently, physicians rely on subjective judgment to assess readmission risk. [cite_start]Important indicators like HbA1c levels and medication changes are often underutilized, leading to inconsistent discharge planning[cite: 80].

We developed a Data-Driven Decision Support System (DSS) that integrates patient demographics, clinical history, and treatment data to forecast 30-day readmission risk, enabling targeted interventions before discharge.

Methodology & Data Engineering

[cite_start]We utilized the "Diabetes 130-US Hospitals" dataset (1999-2008) from the UCI Machine Learning Repository[cite: 163]. The pipeline involved extensive preprocessing to handle missing data and class imbalance.

Key Features Extracted

  • Demographics: Age group, Gender, Weight availability.
    • [cite_start]
  • Utilization: Number of Inpatient, Emergency, and Outpatient visits[cite: 171].
  • Clinical: HbA1c results, Time in Hospital, Number of Lab Procedures, Number of Medications.

Model Selection

We evaluated Logistic Regression, Random Forest, and Gradient Boosting. [cite_start]Gradient Boosting was selected as the champion model for its superior performance (Validation AUC: 0.694) and ability to handle non-linear interactions[cite: 127].

Sensitivity & "What-If" Analysis

To validate the model's robustness, we conducted a "What-If" analysis to observe how changes in hospital utilization affect risk scores. This transparency builds clinician trust in the AI system.

Case Study: Patient 1303

Patient 1303 had high utilization (8 inpatient visits, 10 emergency visits). [cite_start]The model correctly identified this patient as High Risk with a 57.49% readmission probability[cite: 252].

[cite_start]Critical Insight: Our sensitivity analysis indicated an increase in readmission probability of ~2.1% for every day decrease in length of stay, highlighting the danger of premature discharge[cite: 24].

Interface & Implementation (PEIT)

We deployed the model using a Streamlit web interface backed by a PostgreSQL database. [cite_start]The design prioritizes "Actionable Intelligence"[cite: 132].
