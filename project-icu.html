Synthetic Time Series Data Generation

Creating Virtual Twins for ICU Patient Monitoring
April 2023 Python, TensorFlow, GANs, Autoencoders Role: Algorithm Developer

The Challenge: Data Scarcity

In healthcare, collecting large-scale, high-quality patient data is notoriously difficult due to privacy regulations (HIPAA/GDPR) and the high cost of annotation. This scarcity hinders the training of robust deep learning models for critical tasks like arrhythmia detection in ICUs.

Project Goal: "To generate high-fidelity synthetic time-series data (ECG signals) using Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), effectively creating 'virtual twins' that can be used for training without compromising patient privacy."

By augmenting real datasets with synthetic ones, we aim to improve the generalization capabilities of diagnostic models in data-scarce environments.

GAN Architecture & Methodology

We implemented a Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) specifically adapted for time-series data. The system consists of two competing neural networks:

  • The Generator: Takes random noise vectors as input and attempts to construct realistic ECG waveforms that mimic the statistical properties of the training set.
  • The Discriminator: A binary classifier that evaluates input signals and tries to distinguish between "Real" (from the dataset) and "Fake" (from the Generator).

Optimization Strategy

We utilized the Adam Optimizer with a learning rate of 1.5e-4 and a beta value of 0.5 to ensure stable convergence during the adversarial training process.

Training & Loss Functions

The training process involves a zero-sum game where the Generator minimizes the probability of the Discriminator catching a fake, while the Discriminator maximizes its detection accuracy.

Loss Calculation

We implemented Binary Cross-Entropy Loss to quantify the performance of both networks:

  • Discriminator Loss: real_loss + fake_loss. It penalizes the model when it misclassifies a real image as fake or a fake image as real.
  • Generator Loss: Calculated based on the Discriminator's ability to detect fakes. The Generator is rewarded when the Discriminator mistakenly classifies a fake image as real.
Code Implementation: 
def discriminator_loss(real_output, fake_output):
    real_loss = cross_entropy(tf.ones_like(real_output), real_output)
    fake_loss = cross_entropy(tf.zeros_like(fake_output), fake_output)
    return real_loss + fake_loss

Results & Strategic Impact

The project successfully demonstrated that GANs can generate high-fidelity synthetic data for ICU monitoring.

Key Outcomes:

  • Data Augmentation: Created a virtually infinite stream of synthetic ECG data, allowing for the training of deeper, more complex neural networks without overfitting.
  • Privacy Preservation: The synthetic data retains the statistical properties of the patient population but contains no personally identifiable information (PII), resolving HIPAA constraints.
  • Robustness: Models trained on the augmented dataset (Real + Synthetic) showed improved generalization when tested on unseen real-world data.
