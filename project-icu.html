Synthetic ICU Data Generation

Addressing Data Scarcity with Time-Series GANs
Fall 2024 Python, TensorFlow, TimeGAN Role: AI Researcher

The Challenge: Data Privacy

Medical research is often bottlenecked by strict privacy regulations (HIPAA) and data scarcity. Real-world ICU data is highly sensitive, making it difficult to share for training robust Machine Learning models.

Solution: "Generate high-fidelity synthetic time-series data that preserves the statistical properties of real patient vitals without compromising patient privacy."

We focused on generating synthetic signals for Heart Rate, Blood Pressure, and SpO2 using a Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) framework.

Model Architecture: TimeGAN

Standard GANs struggle with the temporal correlations found in time-series data. We implemented TimeGAN, which introduces an embedding network to learn the underlying dynamics.

Key Components

  • Embedding Function: Maps static and temporal features into a latent space.
  • Recovery Function: Reconstructs the data from the latent space (Autoencoder loss).
  • Generator & Discriminator: Compete to produce realistic sequences (Adversarial loss).

Training Dynamics

The training process involves a "Min-Max" game. The Generator tries to create fake vitals that look real, while the Discriminator tries to distinguish between real ICU data and the synthetic output.

Supervised Loss: Unlike vanilla GANs, we added a supervised loss component to force the model to capture the step-by-step temporal transition of vital signs (e.g., if BP drops, Heart Rate might spike).

Figure 4: TSTR Evaluation – Classifying patient outcomes using only Synthetic Data.

Validation: TSTR Analysis

To prove the utility of our synthetic data, we used the Train on Synthetic, Test on Real (TSTR) methodology.

Results

We trained a classifier solely on our generated synthetic data and tested it on real hold-out patient data.

  • Fidelity: The synthetic data successfully captured the diverse patterns of ICU vitals.
  • Utility: The classifier achieved near-identical accuracy (within 2%) compared to a model trained on real data, proving the synthetic data is a viable substitute for research.
